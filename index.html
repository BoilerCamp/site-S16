ABOUT

Hackathons have the power to change lives. But there's one issue we have yet to solve: how to make these events accessible to anyone, especially beginners. Most beginners are lost when they attend their first hackathon. When you pass someone working on some crazy robotic arm it's easy to think that you don't belong. That sucks. This year, we are introducing a new event called BoilerCamp. It's a day long workshop geared towards teaching you how to make your own project from start to finish. We'll have mentors, swag, food and everything else you need to begin your hackathon career.

FAQ

Who can attend BoilerCamp?

BoilerCamp is for any Purdue University undergraduate student, with little to no experience in programming. You can be in any major— all that we ask is that you want to learn.

I want to attend! What should I know?

Sign up here. We will provide updates in the coming weeks.

Is this a hackathon?

No, BoilerCamp is a one day program aimed at preparing you for your first hackathon.

How many people get accepted?

Everyone. There is no limit beyond the capacity of the venue.

What should I bring?

Bring your computer, charger, phone and a notebook. We'll take care of everything else.

Where is BoilerCamp?

We have yet to finalize a location but it'll be somewhere on campus, we'll keep you updated.

Do I have to attend the whole day?

While it's not required we highly recommend that you stay present throughout the entire event.

How much does it cost?

Nothing. It's on the house.
